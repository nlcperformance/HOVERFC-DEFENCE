The Hannibal Defence System

System Status: Active

Primary Aims

Suffocate the opposition with extreme mental and physical pressure via technical mastery.

Metrics for Success

If we concede less points, we force the opposition to make more errors.

They turn on each other
They physically fade
They will kick... and we'll be ready.

Known Weaknesses & Threats

Disconnections
Individual Errors Tech misses, mismatches, poor tackle choice, bottle.
Cross-field Kicks Vulnerable on the edges.
Silent Back 3 Lack of communication from the rear.

Execution Strategy (The "How")

  • 1

    Split defending into 3 specific ranges (Short, Medium, Long).

  • 2

    Ensure all ranges are explored during every training session.

  • 3

    These ranges are then rigorously tested across the 3 zones of the pitch (Red, Transition, Score/Green).

The 3 Ranges Defined

Phase 1 Short Range (Face to Face)

Focus on grapple/tackle techniques and building absolute collision confidence.

Phase 2 Medium Range (5-10m)

Applying techniques to master the system. Key elements include:

Nause (slowing the 9) Doubles Wolf Scout Back 3 Flag (chosen pre-game)

Phase 3 Long Range

Focus on defence strategy specific to each pitch zone:

Red Zone
Transition
Green (22 to try line)
